What is SADC Digital Currency?

The SADC Digital Currency is a secure and government-backed digital legal tender issued by the regional central banks of the Southern African Development Community. It is designed for everyday use by the general public, offering fast, safe, and reliable digital payments across borders.

Note: The SADC Digital Currency is not a cryptocurrency. It is a centralized digital version of fiat currency, fully regulated and backed 1:1 by sovereign assets.

Key Features

  • Legal tender, backed by participating central banks
  • 24/7 availability and real-time transfers
  • Offline payment support in remote areas
  • Interoperable with all major commercial banks
  • Zero-fee personal transactions
  • Secure with biometric authentication

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Who can use the SADC Digital Currency?
Any citizen or resident of a participating country with a national ID or mobile number can onboard via the wallet app.
2. Is it a form of cryptocurrency?
No. It is a centralized, government-backed digital currency, not a decentralized crypto asset.
3. Do I need a bank account to use it?
No. You can use your mobile phone and national ID to access the CBDC wallet directly, even without a bank account.
4. Is it safe to use?
Yes. The CBDC is protected using government-grade security, including biometric login and AI-based fraud detection.
5. What can I use it for?
You can use it for shopping, bills, person-to-person transfers, government disbursements, and more.
